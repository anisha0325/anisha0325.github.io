  • Experience and Evidence are the eyes of an excellent summarizer! Towards Knowledge Infused Multi-modal Clinical Conversation Summarization
    ACM Conference on Information and Knowledge Management (CIKM'23)

    In this paper, we first present a multi- modal clinical conversation summary generation task that takes a clinician-patient interaction (both textual and visual information) and generates a succinct synopsis of the conversation. We propose a knowledge-infused, multi-modal, multi-tasking medical domain identification and clinical conversation summary generation (MM- CliConSummation) framework. It leverages an adapter to infuse knowledge and visual features and unify the fused feature vector us- ing a gated mechanism. Furthermore, we developed a multi-modal, multi-intent clinical conversation summarization corpus annotated with intent, symptom, and summary. The extensive set of experi- ments, both quantitatively and qualitatively, led to the following findings: (a) critical significance of visuals, (b) more precise and medical entity preserving summary with additional knowledge infusion, and (c) a correlation between medical department identi- fication and clinical synopsis generation.
  • Guide to Distributed Representations in ML
    Analytics India Magazine

    The article focuses on how to construct Distributed Representations (DR), a look into the Word2Vec model, the various applications of DR and demonstration of building DR using deep learning techniques, on a classification task for sentiment analysis.

  • Hierarchical Attention based Code Summarization and Relevance
    We aim to apply Hierarchical Attention Networks (HAN) for encoding the hierarchical structure of code source , which has been previously employed in the tasks of log message prediction, bug-fixing patch identification and just-in-time defect prediction. The goal is to investigate whether HAN performs better as an encoder, than any state-of-the-art models, even with a vanilla decoder.
  • Medical Concern Summary Generation
    In this work, we investigate the effectiveness of a multi-faceted approach that simultaneously produces summaries of medical con- cerns, doctor impressions, and an overall view. We aim to build a multi-tasking, multi-modal, knowledge-infused summary generation (MMK-Summation) model, which takes dialogues as input, extracts pertinent external knowl- edge based on the context, integrates the knowledge and vi- sual cues from the dialogues into the textual content, and ul- timately generates concise summaries encompassing medical concerns, doctor impressions, and a comprehensive overview.
